Aquatic Invertebrates


Giant Squid


-*Characteristics*: Known for their massive size, giant squids can reach up to 40 feet in length. They have large eyes (the largest in the animal kingdom) to see in deep ocean darkness and long tentacles with sharp suckers for capturing prey. - *Habitat*: Deep oceans around the world. - *Interesting Fact*: Giant squids are elusive and were first captured on video in their natural habitat only in 2012.


Moon Jellyfish (Aurelia aurita)


-*Characteristics*: Moon jellyfish are translucent with a slight pink or blue tinge and are named for their moon-like shape. They feed on plankton, which they catch with their tentacles. - *Habitat*: Oceans worldwide, primarily in shallow coastal areas. - *Interesting Fact*: Moon jellyfish can survive in low-oxygen environments, which makes them resilient in polluted waters.


Blue-ringed Octopus


- *Characteristics*: Despite being small (about the size of a golf ball), these octopuses are one of the most venomous animals. They have blue rings on their bodies that flash as a warning when threatened. - *Habitat*: Shallow waters in the Pacific and Indian Oceans, especially around Australia and Japan. - *Interesting Fact*: Their venom contains a potent neurotoxin, and there is no known antidote.


Sea Cucumber


- *Characteristics*: Sea cucumbers have soft, elongated bodies and are essential for ocean ecosystems as they recycle nutrients by breaking down detritus on the ocean floor. - *Habitat*: Found in oceans worldwide, especially on coral reefs and the sea floor. - *Interesting Fact*: When threatened, some sea cucumbers expel their internal organs to distract predators—a process they can later regenerate.


Pistol Shrimp


- *Characteristics*: Pistol shrimp have a specialized claw that snaps shut so quickly it creates a cavitation bubble, producing sound waves capable of stunning or killing prey. - *Habitat*: Warm, shallow waters, especially in coral reefs. - *Interesting Fact*: The snapping sound produced by pistol shrimp can reach up to 210 decibels, making it one of the loudest sounds in the ocean.
