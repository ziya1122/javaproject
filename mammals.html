Aquatic Mammals


Dolphin


- Dolphins are highly social, intelligent marine mammals found in oceans worldwide. - They communicate through clicks, whistles, and body language and use echolocation to navigate and find food. - Known for their playful nature, dolphins are often seen riding waves and interacting with boats.


Whales


- Whales are the largest mammals on Earth, with some species, like the blue whale, reaching lengths of up to 100 feet. - They are known for their complex vocalizations, particularly in species like humpback whales. - Whales play vital roles in marine ecosystems by helping regulate the populations of their prey species.


Sea Otter


- Sea otters are small marine mammals found along the coasts of the northern and eastern North Pacific Ocean. - Known for using rocks to break open shellfish, they are one of the few animals that use tools. - Sea otters play an essential role in maintaining the health of kelp forests by controlling sea urchin populations.


Manatee


- Also known as "sea cows," manatees are herbivorous mammals found in shallow, warm coastal waters and rivers. - They are slow-moving and primarily feed on seagrass and other aquatic plants. - Manatees are gentle creatures and are considered vulnerable due to habitat loss and boat collisions.


Walrus


- Walruses are large, tusked marine mammals that live in the Arctic region. - They use their long tusks to haul themselves out of the water and to defend themselves from predators. - Walruses primarily feed on shellfish, especially clams, which they suck out of the shells with their powerful mouths.
