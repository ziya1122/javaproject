Aquatic Reptiles


Salt Water Crocodile


- *Habitat*: Found in brackish and freshwater habitats across Southeast Asia and northern Australia. - *Size*: Can grow over 20 feet long, making it the largest living reptile. - *Diet*: Apex predator feeding on fish, birds, and mammals. - *Unique Trait*: Known for powerful bite strength and territorial behavior.


Green Sea Turtle


- -*Habitat*: Tropical and subtropical seas around the world. - *Diet*: Primarily herbivorous, feeding on seagrass and algae. - *Lifespan*: Can live up to 80 years. - *Unique Trait*: Known for long migrations between feeding and nesting grounds.


Nile Crocodile


- *Habitat*: Freshwater rivers, lakes, and marshes in Africa. - *Size*: Can reach lengths of up to 16 feet. - *Diet*: Feeds on fish, birds, and mammals, often ambushing prey from the water. - *Unique Trait*: Known for cooperative hunting behavior.


Marine Iguana


- *Habitat*: Found only on the Galápagos Islands. - *Diet*: Herbivorous, mainly feeding on marine algae. - *Unique Trait*: The only marine lizard, capable of diving up to 30 feet to feed. - *Adaptation*: Can expel salt from glands near its nose.


American Alligator


- *Habitat*: Freshwater habitats in the southeastern United States. - *Diet*: Carnivorous, eating fish, birds, and small mammals. - *Size*: Can grow up to 15 feet. - *Unique Trait*: Known for its bellowing sound, especially during mating season.
