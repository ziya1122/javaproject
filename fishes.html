Aquatic Fishes


Clownfish


- *Famous For*: Their vibrant orange and white stripes and symbiotic relationship with sea anemones. - *Habitat*: Coral reefs, especially in the Indian and Pacific Oceans. - *Interesting Fact: Made famous by the movie *Finding Nemo, clownfish are born male and can change to female if necessary, helping to maintain their group structure.


Great White Shark


- *Famous For: Known as one of the ocean's top predators and popularized by the movie *Jaws. - *Habitat*: Coastal waters worldwide, especially around South Africa, Australia, and California. - *Interesting Fact*: Great whites have powerful senses, particularly smell, which allows them to detect a single drop of blood in vast ocean waters.


Goldfish


- *Famous For*: One of the most popular aquarium fish in the world. - *Habitat*: Originally from slow-moving waters in East Asia; now bred worldwide in aquariums. - *Interesting Fact*: Despite myths, goldfish have a memory span of months and can be trained to recognize their owners or perform tricks.


Bettafish


- *Famous For*: Known for their bright colors and flowing fins, as well as their aggressive behavior toward other males. - *Habitat*: Native to shallow waters in Southeast Asia, especially rice paddies and canals. - *Interesting Fact*: Betta fish are also called “Siamese fighting fish” because males will fiercely defend their territory.


Blue Tang


- *Famous For: Their striking blue color with a yellow tail and their character "Dory" in *Finding Nemo and Finding Dory. - *Habitat*: Coral reefs in the Indo-Pacific region. - *Interesting Fact*: Blue tangs can secrete a toxin from their skin as a defense against predators, making them resilient in the wild.
